Questions on the cards

The questions come from three different sources: questions that interviewers look for on Google, questions that interviewers asked me on my interviews, and questions constantly reported by other developers.

Questions that the interviewers search in Google

8 out of 10 interviewers I asked confessed that they googled the phrase “frontend interview questions” or “js interview questions” and later asked the questions during the interview. I even did it myself when I was interviewing people for our company!

Questions that the interviewers kept asking me

I went to about 50 interviews myself to see if there’s a pattern to which questions the interviewers in various companies like to ask. At some point, the interviews became rather boring because they all repeated themselves, I got a lot of questions that were EXACTLY the same.

Questions from the other developers

I have a huge network of friends who got hired in great companies, and they regularly deliver me new questions. A lot of the stuff that the interviewer want you to know is the same from company to company.

  • Q: What don't you create a mobile app with these questions?

    A: The paper form has its own advantages. Mainly it's the fact that you can see more than one card at the same time. For example, you can put 50 cards on a table to quickly review your knowledge. Some of my clients use the cards during the interviews when they need to pick a good candidate in their teams.
